Grammar: Second Conditional

We use the Second Conditional to talk about imaginary, impossible, or unlikely situations in the present or future.

Condition (If + Past)

"If the world was ending..."

This is the imaginary situation. The world is NOT ending right now.

Result (Would + Verb)

"I would want to be next to you."

This is what you would do in that imaginary situation.

Fact vs. Imagination

See how a real fact changes into an imaginary dream using If + Past and Would.

Click the button to dream

I don't have a million dollars. I don't buy a house. (Fact: Real life right now.)
If I had a million dollars, I would buy a house! (Imagination: A dream situation.)

Grammar Drill 1

Remember: The 'If' part uses the Past Tense!

"If I _______ you, I would smile more."

Note: In the Second Conditional, we usually use "were" for all subjects (If I were, If he were), but "was" is also very common in songs (like "If the world was ending").
